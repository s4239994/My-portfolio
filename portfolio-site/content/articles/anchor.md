Summary: A personal safety-planning app for hard moments -- build your plan while steady, reach it instantly when you're not.

A personal safety-planning app, built for one person's own use, on their
own device. You build your plan while you're steady; the app makes it
instantly reachable when you're not.

## How it works

- **Build My Plan** -- pick a metaphor for how a hard day feels, write
  your own warning signs and coping strategies, add trusted contacts,
  list reasons to stay and what you're working toward, leave yourself a
  message for later.
- **Right Now** (crisis mode) -- one thing at a time: a breathing pacer,
  then your own words back to you, then your reasons, then one-tap
  contact with a person or a crisis line. The visual metaphor you picked
  actually calms down as you move through it -- a real technique
  (narrative externalization) made visible instead of static.
- **Crisis Lines, My People, Breathe, My History, Turned Into** -- always
  one tap from real help, private mood tracking for your own reflection,
  and a separate space for meaning-making *after* a hard stretch, not
  instead of getting through it.

## What this deliberately doesn't do

No activity tracking of any kind, no AI in crisis mode (that page is
100% pre-written by the user, static, predictable), and no
brainwave-science claims on the optional ambient sound. It's a bridge to
real help, never a replacement for it -- see the [project README](https://github.com/s4239994/My-portfolio/tree/main/anchor)
for the full reasoning and real crisis line numbers.

## Stack

Python, Streamlit, SQLite -- entirely local, never deployed publicly,
given how personal the data it holds is.
