The process

Four steps between a Pinterest board and a finished post.

Each trend drop goes through the same pipeline before it reaches your Canva sidebar — curated by people, structured by AI, finished by you.

01

A trend is spotted and verified

A small editorial team (not a scraper) tracks aesthetic cycles across fashion, interiors, and content culture — the same way a trend forecaster works. Each candidate is checked against real usage before it qualifies: is this actually circulating, or just one viral post? Verified trends get named, dated, and documented.

02

The aesthetic gets decomposed

Reference imagery for the trend is broken down into its actual components: a 4–6 color palette pulled to real hex values, a font pairing that matches its mood (serif and expressive vs. grotesque and clean), and a layout style — dense collage vs. generous whitespace, for example.

example decomposition: Dark Academia

03

Your brand kit and the trend are merged

If you have an existing Canva brand kit, Magic Trends doesn't just hand you the raw trend palette — it finds the overlap between what's on-trend and what's already yours, so the result still looks like your brand wearing this season's aesthetic, not a template that happens to be nearby.

04

It opens straight in the editor

No export, no re-upload. The finished layout opens as a normal, fully editable Canva design — every layer separate, exactly like Magic Design's output today — so you're editing text and swapping photos within seconds of opening it, not starting from a blank page.

A note on sourcing

Magic Trends never scrapes TikTok, Instagram, or Pinterest directly — all three explicitly prohibit it in their terms of service. Trend curation here is editorial: a documented, human-verified process, the same way a fashion trend report is written, not an automated feed.
